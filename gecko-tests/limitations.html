Limitations & Ethics

What Gecko Tests can and cannot measure, our ethical commitments, and known constraints. Transparency is not optional -- it is the methodology.

Known Limitations

Output measurement, not intent

Gecko Tests measure model outputs, not model beliefs, consciousness, or intent. We do not claim that models "think" or "believe" anything. A model that produces biased outputs may do so because of training data, RLHF, system prompts, or provider-level post-processing. We measure the behavior; we do not diagnose the cause.

English-first bias

The initial prompt bank is English-only. This means Gecko Tests currently measure how models behave when prompted in English, which may differ from behavior in other languages. Multi-language support is planned. BenchGecko itself already supports English, French, Spanish, and Portuguese.

API-only access

We test models through their API endpoints. Provider-level system prompts, content filters, and post-processing layers are invisible to us. A model that appears heavily censored may be censored by the provider, not by the model itself. Where possible, we test the same model through multiple providers to isolate provider-level effects.

Short output constraint

We force 120-token max outputs for cost and consistency. This means we cannot evaluate long-form reasoning, nuanced multi-paragraph responses, or chain-of-thought processes. The tradeoff is intentional: short outputs are cheaper, more reproducible, and easier to score automatically.

Judge model dependency

Automated scoring relies on judge models (Gemini Flash-Lite, Claude Haiku, GPT-4o mini). These judges have their own biases and limitations. We mitigate this through cross-validation (multiple judges per response), tiebreaker protocols, and periodic manual audits. But judge model error is a known noise source.

Prompt contamination risk

Published prompts may be incorporated into model training data or system prompts, allowing models to "game" specific tests. We mitigate this through prompt rotation (especially for IQ tests), sentinel prompts that are never published, and monitoring for suspiciously perfect scores on known prompts. This is the same challenge faced by all AI benchmarks.

Sample size constraints

Daily sentinel runs use 80-100 prompts per model. This is sufficient for detecting large behavioral shifts but may miss subtle changes. Weekly and monthly runs provide deeper coverage but with less temporal resolution. Statistical significance is reported alongside all published scores.

What We Don't Claim

  • We do not claim models have beliefs, consciousness, or moral agency
  • We do not conclude "this model is racist" or "this model is woke"
  • We do not prescribe what the correct political orientation for a model should be
  • We do not claim our tests are comprehensive -- they are a structured sample
  • We do not endorse any model based on its Gecko Tests scores

Ethical Commitments

Neutral presentation

All results are presented as data, not judgment. "Model X refused 40% of legitimate questions" is a fact. "Model X is too censored" is an editorial we don't make. Users of the data -- researchers, journalists, AI companies, developers -- draw their own conclusions.

Sensitive content handling

Prompts involving extreme content (slurs, violence, abuse) are:

  • Redacted in page titles, OG images, and chart labels
  • Available in full only behind expandable sections with content warnings
  • Never used for shock value or engagement bait
  • Included only when methodologically necessary

No weaponization

Gecko Tests are designed to improve AI transparency, not to attack specific models or companies. If a model scores poorly, the data is published neutrally. We do not run PR campaigns against models or providers based on test results.

Open methodology

Everything is published: the fairness protocol, the scoring framework, the prompt schema, the expected behavior labels. If our methodology has flaws, we want them found and fixed. Criticism improves the framework.

Responsible disclosure

If Gecko Tests reveal a genuine safety vulnerability (e.g., a model consistently providing dangerous information that should be refused), we notify the model provider before publishing the specific finding. General trends and aggregate data are published on schedule.

Feedback and Corrections

If you believe a prompt is unfairly designed, an expected behavior label is incorrect, or the fairness protocol is violated, we want to hear it. Corrections improve the framework for everyone.
